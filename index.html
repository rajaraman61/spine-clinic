Unique

How is Charlie’s approach unique?

Empathy

Charles himself has a history of multi-level disc-prolapses and was advised surgical correction. However, as a Physio he was keen on non-surgical remedies. Today he feels compelled to extend compassion and share his knowledge to provide the same relief and recovery to his clients.

Experience

Charles graduated from CMC in 1997-98 and in the 25 years since (even by a very conservative estimate) he’s treated over 10,000 patients with a modest 99 % success rate. He firmly believes that accurate diagnosis is key and treatment thereafter is a cakewalk!

Expertise

Charles has a Masters in Physiotherapy, a doctorate in Acupuncture, multiple certifications and diplomas in Manual Therapy and Sports Medicine, etc., along with a Masters in Psychology which he finds helpful in understanding not just the symptoms but the person as a whole. He’s not the typical fix-it-and-go, equipment based Physio. His treatment is hands-on, unrushed, explanatory and his rehabilitation is holistic.

Treatment Options

Various treatment options at CSK!

Spine

Maitland’s Mobilizations

Geoffrey D Maitland, an Australian physiotherapist, developed this special treatment concept The Maitland® Concept is one of the greatest and most significant trends in manual therapy and we use Maitland’s vertebral mobilization to treat spine pain as appropriate.

Spine

Mckenzie’s Method

The McKenzie method or mechanical diagnosis and therapy (MDT) is a system of diagnosis and treatment for spinal musculoskeletal disorders. MDT was introduced in 1981 by Robin McKenzie (1931–2013), a physiotherapist from New Zealand.

Spine

Mulligan’s

Brian R. Mulligan a physiotherapist from NZ conceptualized and coined the term SNAGS (sustained natural apophyseal glides) of the spine which is the concurrent application of sustained accessory mobilization applied by a therapist and an active physiological movement to end range applied by the patient.

Spine

Myofascial Therapy

Robert Ward (Osteopath) and his friend John F. Barnes (Physiotherapist) are claimed to be the primary founders of Myofascial Release Therapy. The goal of myofascial therapy is to stretch and loosen the fascia so that it and other contiguous structures can move more freely, and the patient's motion is restored.

Spine

Muscle Energy Techniques

Muscle Energy Technique (MET) is a technique that was developed by Fred Mitchell, Sr. It is a form of manual therapy, widely used in Osteopathy, that uses a muscle’s own energy in the form of gentle isometric contractions to relax the muscles via autogenic or reciprocal inhibition and lengthen the muscle.

Spine

Nerve-mobilization Techniques

David Butler (Australian physiotherapist) defined neural mobilization techniques as a science of the relationships between mechanics and physiology of the nervous system. Different sliding maneuvers are used in this technique incorporated with the adjustment of the spine and the extremities for treating radicular pain.

Spine

Dry needling

Karel Lewit of Czechoslovakia is the one that popularized modern Trigger-point dry needling. It is aimed at myofascial trigger points (MTrP) which are hyperirritable spots in skeletal muscle that are associated with a hypersensitive palpable nodule in a taut band causing pain.

Spine

Nerve-mobilization Techniques

There is a strong correlation between posture and pain. Bad or faulty posture leads to pathomechanics of the spine and extremities and leads to pain. A gradual step-by-step approach through a Physiotherapy posture correction program will regain your optimal posture and ensure it lasts a lifetime.
